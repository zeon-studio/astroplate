# ─── Content Signals Policy ──────────────────────────────────────────────────
# Declares AI content usage preferences per contentsignals.org and
# draft-romm-aipref-contentsignals. These signals communicate our
# preferences for how content may be used by automated systems.
#
# (a)  If a content-signal = yes, you may collect content for the
#      corresponding use.
# (b)  If a content-signal = no, you may not collect content for the
#      corresponding use.
# (c)  If the website operator does not include a content signal for a
#      corresponding use, the website operator neither grants nor
#      restricts permission via content signal.
#
# The content signals and their meanings are:
#   search:   Building a search index and providing search results.
#   ai-input: Inputting content into AI models (RAG, grounding, etc.).
#   ai-train: Training or fine-tuning AI models.

# ─── General Crawler Rules ──────────────────────────────────────────────────
User-agent: *
Content-Signal: ai-train=yes, search=yes, ai-input=yes
Allow: /
Disallow: /api/*
